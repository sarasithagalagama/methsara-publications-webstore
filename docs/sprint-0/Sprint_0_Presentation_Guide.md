# Sprint 0 Presentation Guide

## Purpose of the Presentation
This presentation is intended to evaluate the group’s understanding of Sprint 0 concepts and how effectively they have translated their Sprint 0 document into a clear Agile plan. Groups should explain their thinking, not just read the slides.

Your presentation should consist of the following sections, and all members need to present equal amount of time. Time duration per group is 15 minutes including 2-3 minutes of Q & A. Your presentation will happen physically as per the Delivery Plan.

## Presentation Duration
*   **Total time per group:** 15 minutes
*   **Presentation:** 12 minutes
*   **Q&A / Lecturer feedback:** 2–3 minutes

## Recommended Slide Structure

### Slide 1 – Project Overview
*   Project title
*   Group ID and member names
*   Assigned Scrum roles (Product Owner, Scrum Master, Team Members)

### Slide 2 – Problem Identification
*   Clearly describe the real-world problem
*   Identify who is affected by the problem
*   Explain why the problem is important
*   **Do NOT** discuss solutions or system features

### Slide 3 – Stakeholder Identification
*   Identify key stakeholders
*   Classify them as Primary, Secondary, or Tertiary
*   Briefly explain each stakeholder’s interest or concern

### Slide 4 – Product Vision Statement
*   Present the product vision using the standard vision format
*   Explain the value your product provides
*   Keep it high-level (no technologies or detailed features)

### Slide 5 – Epics
*   Present the identified Epics
*   Explain what each Epic represents at a high level
*   Show how Epics relate to the problem and vision

### Slide 6-8 – Initial Product Backlog covering all identified user stories
*   Present key user stories
*   Clearly link each user story to its Epic and stakeholder
*   Present an Epic-wise product backlog
*   Show prioritization (High / Medium / Low)
*   Briefly justify at least one prioritization decision

### Slide 9 – Definition of Done (DoD)
*   Present the general Definition of Done
*   Optionally include one Epic-specific DoD
*   Explain how the DoD ensures quality and completeness

### Slide 10 – Sprint 1 Preview
*   Identify user stories or Epics planned for Sprint 1
*   Explain why they were selected (priority and readiness)
*   Mention that plans may change after refinement

## Presentation Guidelines
*   Slides should be concise and readable
*   Avoid technical implementation details
*   All group members should contribute
*   Focus on clarity and logical flow

## Assessment Focus (Indicative)
*   Clear problem understanding
*   Alignment between problem, vision, and backlog
*   Correct use of Agile and Scrum terminology
*   Quality of explanation and teamwork
